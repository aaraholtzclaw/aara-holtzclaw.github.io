BUSINESS SUMMARY

 

The U.S. Sugar Association (The Sugar Association) is a non-profit organization geared towards informing the public of sugars importance in healthy dieting. In 1943 members of the U.S. sugar industry founded the Sugar Association. The idea for the organization emerged from the Sugar Research Foundation. Originally the purpose was to study sugars role in food and to communicate that role to the masses. In 1947 a keen interest was found in the study, the association assumed its current name.The Sugar Association initially had two divisions of operation. The Sugar Information Inc. focused on public awareness while the Sugar Research Foundation developed and supported the scientific aspect of sugar. Today the organization continues to move forward with its mission of educating and bringing awareness to health professionals, government officials, media, and the public about the wonders of sugar. We have put together several communication initiatives to attract people of all kinds to implement sugar into their diets and understand the truth about sugar. 

 

VISION STATEMENT - To remove the negative stigma about sugar from the average consumers mind. 

 

MISSION STATEMENT -Promoting the consumption of sugar through sound scientific principles while maintaining an understanding of the benefits that sugar contributes to the quality of wholesome foods and beverages.

SITUATION ANALYSIS

 

After the recent premiere of health documentary, Fed Up the U.S. Sugar Association is in a position to defend its brand against protesters and lobbyists who are claiming that sugar is harmful for the body. In the media, many talk show host and healthy lifestyle outlets are advocating for less sugar consumption. While too much of anything is harmful to the body, The U.S. Sugar Association would like to advocate for sweet treats that have been a part of Americas culture for years, and remind citizens that sugar is fine if consumed in moderation. We would also like to remind Americans that sugar substitutes like Truvia and Splenda are more expensive and may even contain more chemicals and unknown ingredients.

EXTERNAL ENVIRONMENT

 

The increase in demand for sugar has significantly impacted the economy. Along with the increase in production, consumption has increased tremendously. Sugar has become a necessity to households across the nation. More resources must now be expended in order to meet the needs of the growing industry. More and more forests are being cleared to plant sugar. In order to process the sugar, wood or fossil fuel is needed which ultimately has harmful effects on the environment. However, the boom of the sugar industry has created opportunities for employment.

 

Inversely the consumption of sugar continues to rise but consumer needs arent reflecting such. Consumers desire less sugar because individuals prefer to leave a healthy lifestyle than in the past. Other consumer trends that impact the sugar industry and The Sugar Association from its mission is the increase in artificial preservatives. Lastly parallel consumption trends occur by consuming other products such as coffee, tea, and chocolate which makes advocates for sugar jobs a difficult .

COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE

 

●      Our competitors include; Splenda, Truvia, Agave Nectar, Honey, Stevia and Sugar In The Raw

●      These sugar substitutes are our competitors because they have one of more intriguing factors: zero calories, no jumps in blood sugar levels, increase in insulin levels which is beneficial to diabetic consumers, may come directly from plants or are unbleached (natural), provide a boost in vitamins and minerals or have a low glycemic index.

●      The market leadership is currently owned by sugar replacements like Splenda and Truvia due to their individual packets and powder/grainy form which is able to be taken on the go our in the comfort of ones home. Agave Nectar and Honey are still followed closely. These type of sweeteners come in different varieties such as light, dark etc. Also, Sugar In The Raw provides customers with raw sugar (unbleached).

 

 

COMMUNICATIONS OVERVIEW

 

●      The US Sugar Association want Americans to understand that sugar is not the enemy. The average human does not understand the ingredients that are in their favorite foods. Its our mission to help consumers become knowledgeable about how sugar is used made so they do not think that major companies like The U.S. Sugar Association are implementing unknown variables into their food that they feed their families. We want to create a newer honest company that isnt afraid to explain how things are done and why certain things must be done to sustain the sugar that is used in households daily. While sugar has the reputation of causing weight gain among humans, its also an energy source that fuels your brain and nervous system. With only fifteen calories per teaspoon, sugar is the best sweetener one could incorporate into their diet while maintaining to a healthy weight or lose weight. Replacing sugar with caloric or artificial sweeteners will not aid weight loss in any way because weight loss is directly affected by the amount of calories that are consumed.
